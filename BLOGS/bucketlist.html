The Author's Bucket List

Leave a good legacy

I want to leave behind something that matters. Not just a name in history, but a positive impact that people can feel and remember long after I'm gone.

Learn a lot of skills

I want to be the person that can be anything, a jack of all trades. Being versatile makes life more interesting and less intimidating.

Image Source

Become someone that anyone would admire

I want to be the kind of person that others look up to. Not because of what they do, but because of how they treat others and the values they uphold.

Image Source

Make a good game that everyone would enjoy

I love the idea of creating a game that brings people together. Whether it's a cooperative experience or a competitive one, I want to build something that makes people laugh, think, and connect.

Image Source

Teach someone that would eventually outgrow me

I want to be the kind of person who helps others grow beyond what I ever thought possible. It's not about being the best; it's about being the one who helps others become better than they ever imagined.

Image Source

Be successful in life

Success is not just about reaching a goal; it's about the journey and the growth that comes with it. I want to be successful in a way that makes me proud of who I've become and what I've accomplished.

Image Source

Have a lot of money

I want to be financially secure so that I can focus on what truly matters. Money isn't the end goal, but it's a tool that gives me freedom to pursue my passions and support the people I care about.

Image Source

Travel around the world

I want to see the world from different perspectives. Whether it's the bustling streets of Tokyo, the serene landscapes of New Zealand, or the vibrant culture of Morocco, I want to experience it all.

Image Source

Taste new foods

I want to explore the world through its cuisine. From the spicy flavors of Thailand to the rich pastries of France, I want to savor every bite and discover new tastes that broaden my horizons.

Image Source

Be happy

I want to find joy in the simple things and be content with who I am. Happiness isn't a destination but a way of living that comes from appreciating life's moments and being grateful for what I have.
